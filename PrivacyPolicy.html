Third-Party Services

We use third-party services that may collect information used to identify you:

  1. Google Firebase

      We use Google Firebase to analyze data on how the mobile applications are used. Firebase may collect usage data, device information, and other analytical data. Firebase Analytics enables us to analyze usage and troubleshoot problems. Information (such as device operating system, device name, reason for crash, clicks) on the use of the respective mobile application is transmitted to Google and stored there. This information can be accessed by us and is used to evaluate and optimize the user experience or to deal with technical programming problems. The following data types are recorded in the standard implementation of Google Analytics for Firebase

      1. Number of users
      2. Session duration
      3. Operating system
      4. Device models
      5. Region
      6. First-time launc
      7. App executions
      8. App updates
      9. In-app purchases

      Google uses the advertising ID of the end device for unique identification. You can restrict the use of the advertising ID in the device settings of your iOS device:

      You can restrict the use of the advertising ID on an iOS device in the device settings: Open Settings > Tap Privacy > Select Tracking > Disable the Allow Apps to Request to Track option

      You can also disable Apple's ad targeting by: Navigating to Settings > Privacy > Apple Advertising Setting the Personalized Ads switch to the off position Disabling the ad ID makes it more difficult for advertisers and data brokers to track you. It can also help protect your privacy and make the surveillance advertising industry less profitable. Advertisers use the advertising ID to: Understand the effectiveness of their marketing, Create customer profiles, and Target specific individuals.

      For more information on Firebase’s data collection practices, please refer to the Firebase Privacy Policy Further privacy policy information on Google products can be found here: Google Privacy Policy

      We use Google AdMob to display advertisements within the App. AdMob may collect information about your device and usage patterns to serve personalized ads.

      The information generated (including your IP address) will be transmitted to Google. Google reserves the right to forward the collected data to contractual partners of Google. Google will not associate your IP address with any other information collected from you. The transmitted data, such as the IP address, is required by Google Inc. for commission billing and thus also for the evaluation of the origin of a visitor and the traceability of clicks. For mobile applications, Google uses the advertising ID of the end device to uniquely identify a user. You have the option of deactivating personalized advertising for your device through specific settings.

      You can restrict the use of the advertising ID on an iOS device in the device settings: Open Settings > Tap Privacy > Select Tracking > Disable the Allow Apps to Request to Track option

      You can also disable Apple's ad targeting by: Navigating to Settings > Privacy > Apple Advertising Setting the Personalized Ads switch to the off position Disabling the ad ID makes it more difficult for advertisers and data brokers to track you. It can also help protect your privacy and make the surveillance advertising industry less profitable. Advertisers use the advertising ID to: Understand the effectiveness of their marketing, Create customer profiles, and Target specific individuals.
